Different Approaches to Building Ecosystems

Until 2017, India had only one unicorn. The rapid growth that followed took a different form than in other countries.

While most successful startup ecosystems concentrate in major metropolitan areas, India's unicorns emerged from a diverse range of locations and industries.

This distributed approach to utilizing resources across the country helped India become the world's third-largest unicorn ecosystem.

Key Findings

Two Models of Startup Ecosystems

The USA demonstrates the power of concentrated ecosystems. Deep networks of talent, capital, and expertise in a few key locations.

India shows how distributed ecosystems can also work. Unicorns emerging from multiple states and industries, with less geographic concentration.
